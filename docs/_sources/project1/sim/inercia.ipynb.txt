    "### Moment of Inertia\n",
    "\n",
    "For a **solid disk** of mass $m$ and radius $r$, rotating about its center:\n",
    "\n",
    " $$ \n",
    "I = \\frac{1}{2} m r^2\n",
    " $$ \n",
    "\n",
    "For a **solid wheel** (annular disk) of mass $m$, inner radius $R_i$ and outer radius $R_a$:\n",
    "\n",
    " $$ \n",
    "I = \\frac{1}{2} m (R_i^2 + R_a^2)\n",
    " $$ \n",
    "\n",
    "For a **point mass** $M$ at a distance $L$ from the axis of rotation (e.g., for a pendulum):\n",
    "\n",
    " $$ \n",
    "I = M L^2\n",
    " $$ \n"
   ]

    "\n",
    "For a **general rotationally symmetric object** with radial mass density $\\rho(r)$ (in kg/m):\n",
    "\n",
    " $$ \n",
    "I = \\int_{R_i}^{R_a} \\rho(r) \\, r^2 \\cdot 2\\pi r \\, dr = 2\\pi \\int_{R_i}^{R_a} \\rho(r) \\, r^3 \\, dr\n",
    " $$ \n",
    "\n",
    "For a **composite object** made of multiple components with known moments of inertia $I_1, I_2, \\dots, I_n$:\n",
    "\n",
    " $$ \n",
    "I_{\\text{total}} = I_1 + I_2 + \\dots + I_n = \\sum_{i=1}^n I_i\n",
    " $$ \n",
    "\n",
    "\n",

    "\n",
    "\n",
    "**Measuring Moment of Inertia in the Lab**\n",
    "\n",
    "The **moment of inertia** of an object can be determined experimentally using various methods. One simple and effective method is the **torsional pendulum** or **rotational acceleration method**.\n",
    "\n",
    "**Method 1: Torsional Oscillation**\n",
    "\n",
    "1. **Suspend** the object from a thin wire or torsion spring, allowing it to rotate freely about a vertical axis.\n",
    "2. **Measure the period** $T$ of small-angle oscillations.\n",
    "3. **Determine the torsion constant** $k$ of the wire or system by calibrating with a known inertia or using a reference object.\n",
    "4. Use the formula:\n",
    "5. \n",
    "    $$ \n",
    "   I = \\frac{T^2 k}{4\\pi^2}\n",
    "    $$ \n",
    "\n",
    "   where:\n",
    "   - $I$ is the moment of inertia,\n",
    "   - $T$ is the oscillation period,\n",
    "   - $k$ is the torsional stiffness.\n",
    "\n",
    "**Method 2: Angular Acceleration**\n",
    "\n",
    "1. Apply a **known torque** $\\tau$ (e.g., via a hanging mass and pulley).\n",
    "2. Measure the resulting **angular acceleration** $\\alpha$ (using a rotary encoder or sensor).\n",
    "3. Use Newton's second law for rotation:\n",
    "   \n",
    "    $$ \n",
    "   I = \\frac{\\tau}{\\alpha}\n",
    "    $$ \n",
    "\n",
    "   - $\\tau = r F$, where $F$ is the force applied at radius $r$.\n"
